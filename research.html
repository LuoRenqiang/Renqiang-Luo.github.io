Research

My research lies at the intersection of machine learning methodology and trustworthy AI systems, focusing on robust and scalable learning paradigms for complex data structures and societal applications.
Interested in joining the team? See our Group page for open positions.

🕸️ Theme 1: Trustworthy Graph Learning and GNN Robustness

Graphs are powerful models for real-world interconnected data (social networks, molecules, brain connectivity). My work aims to build scalable and reliable Graph Neural Networks (GNNs) that are robust to noise, adaptable to dynamic changes, and capable of handling heterogeneous information.

  • Key Focus: Designing GNN architectures that maintain performance under adversarial attacks (e.g., node feature perturbation or edge deletion).
  • Recent Projects: Developing Self-Supervised Topology Smoothing for enhancing GNN generalization; Dynamic Graph Evolution Modeling for temporal link prediction.
📃 Related Publications: [Robust GNNs against Adversarial Attacks] | [Spatio-Temporal GCN for Traffic Flow]

⚖️ Theme 2: Algorithmic Fairness and Explainable AI (XAI)

The increasing deployment of AI in high-stakes domains (e.g., finance, healthcare) necessitates a focus on equity and transparency. This research stream develops methods to measure, mitigate, and explain algorithmic bias, ensuring models treat different demographic groups fairly.

  • Key Focus: Defining and enforcing group fairness constraints in graph-structured data and sequential decision-making processes.
  • Recent Projects: Developing Counterfactual Explanations for biased graph classifications; Fairness-Aware Reinforcement Learning for resource allocation.
📃 Related Publications: [Fairness in Graph Social Analysis] | [Interpretable Decisions in RL]

🧠 Theme 3: Large Language Models (LLM) and Knowledge Augmentation

This direction explores methods to enhance the reasoning and knowledge retrieval capabilities of LLMs. We focus on integrating structured knowledge (graphs, databases) to overcome LLMs' reliance on implicit knowledge and their propensity for "hallucination."

  • Key Focus: Using Knowledge Graphs (KGs) to ground LLM outputs and enable complex, multi-hop reasoning.
  • Recent Projects: Graph-Augmented Retrieval-Augmented Generation (G-RAG) for enterprise search; LLM-Based Schema Induction for automated KG construction.
📃 Related Publications: [Graph-RAG: Grounding LLMs with KGs] | [Automated Schema Induction with LLMs]
